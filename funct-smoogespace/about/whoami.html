Notes
Pay scales:
I have always been of the opinion that we do not pay enough to the people who matter the most to our society. These are usually people who work for tax dollars, and get the shaft because no one wants to raise taxes if needed. To misquote someone I heard long ago, you get the society you pay for.
Teachers
While we all see cases of teachers who shouldnt teach... I think that if you look hard enough you will find people in whatever your position is. These people are teaching the future of our society, and usually work a hell of a lot of hours in doing so. (All the teachers I have ever met do 12-14 hour days in preparing classes, dealing with troublesome students, and grading homework.) If we want a better world, these people are at least one third of the answer.
Military Personnel
These people put there lives on the line for the freedoms that you and I take for granted every day. They may be sent to places that we would never want to go, and do things we would never want to do because it is in our countries interests.
Public Peace Officers (Police and Fire)
Again people who do a job that most of us are afraid to do. While this is a difficult job, it is a two way street. Every officer "on the beat" need the respect of the community to do their job, but they also need to make sure they deserve that respect.

There are a lot of discussions from people who feel that one side or another already get paid too much. In the end, I think each of these people have a very tough job, and need as much respect and help as the other.

Bigotry
Bigotry is an ugly disease that I think we are all inflicted with in some way or another. Of all the diseases I have ever had it is the one that I am most sick of. We fear, get angry, and hate each other for things that are so small in differences that they are truly un remarkable. I think I read somewhere that the genetic differences between a black person and a white person are 6 genes... and those genes are so varied in between white populations and black populations that you can't call them markers of superiority, inferiority, or any of the other buzzwords I heard growing up in this country (both North and South of the Mason Dixon line).

In the end, we are all afraid of change and differences probably at some instinctual level. However, we are more afraid of admitting that we have these fears than trying to get past them... so we lie, we enslave, we kill, and we make up excuses that bring out all the ugliness that is human.

If you think I am laying judgement on other people... think again. Most of those words are directed at myself. I have thought unkind thoughts, spoken things I now wish I could wash out with lye, and been a royal bigot just like the rest of us in our own ways...

In the end, I just have to spend every day like the alcoholic working it out and trying to not make the mistakes I made before.

Stephen Smoogen
